About Kerala

Kerala, often referred to as "God's Own Country," is a state in the southern part of India. Known for its palm-lined beaches, backwaters, and network of canals, Kerala is a popular destination for tourists seeking natural beauty and tranquility. The state also boasts a rich cultural heritage, with traditional arts, music, and dance performances being integral parts of its vibrant culture. Kerala's cuisine, known for its use of coconut, spices, and seafood, is another major attraction for visitors.

How to Reach Kerala

Kerala has been long known to be amongst the most accessible locations in the world. Since ancient times, our unique coastline and geographical factors ensured specified trade routes with many civilizations of both the East and the West. With the advent of airways and improvements in rail and road networks, a whole new plethora of routes and passageways have opened to the State. We have provided the links and names to all important airports and railway stations below based on the specific location in the State that you wish to visit. We hope it makes planning your trip to Kerala even easier.

How to Reach Kerala by Air

Kerala has Four major international airports located in Kochi, Trivandrum, Kannur and Calicut. These airports are well-connected to major cities across the globe.

How to Reach Kerala by Train

The Indian Railways network connects Kerala to all major cities in India. Key railway stations include Trivandrum, Ernakulam, and Kozhikode.

How to Reach Kerala by Road

Kerala is connected by an extensive road network to neighboring states. National highways 47, 17, and 49 are the major highways running through the state.
